## Glossary – Key Terms and Acronyms

### A  
- **APT** – Advanced Persistent Threat  
- **AV** – Attack Vector (in CVSS)  

### B  
- **BEC** – Business Email Compromise  
- **Botnet** – Network of compromised devices controlled by an attacker  

### C  
- **C2 (C&C)** – Command and Control (malware communications channel)  
- **CIA** – Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability  
- **CVE** – Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures (public vulnerability ID)  
- **CVSS** – Common Vulnerability Scoring System (vulnerability severity rating)  

### D  
- **DRP** – Disaster Recovery Plan  
- **DDoS** – Distributed Denial of Service  

### E  
- **EDR** – Endpoint Detection and Response  
- **Exploit** – Code that leverages a vulnerability to execute malicious actions  

### F  
- **Fileless Malware** – Malware that resides in memory, not on disk  
- **Firewall** – Network security device that filters traffic  

### G  
- **Grayware** – Software that is annoying or intrusive but not strictly malicious (e.g., adware)  

### H  
- **Honeypot** – Decoy system used to detect attacker activity  

### I  
- **IoC** – Indicator of Compromise  
- **IR** – Incident Response  
- **IRP** – Incident Response Plan  

### K  
- **Keylogger** – Software or hardware that records keystrokes  

### L  
- **LOLBIN** – Living-off-the-Land Binary (legitimate tool abused by malware)  

### M  
- **MFA** – Multi-Factor Authentication  
- **MitM** - Man-in-the-Middle
- **MITRE ATT&CK** – Framework of adversary tactics, techniques, and procedures  

### N  
- **NIST** – National Institute of Standards and Technology  
- **NGFW** – Next-Generation Firewall  
- **NOC** – Network Operations Center  

### O  
- **OSINT** – Open-Source Intelligence  
- **OWASP** – Open Web Application Security Project  

### P  
- **Phishing** – Social engineering attack via deceptive emails or messages  
- **PoC** – Proof of Concept (exploit demonstration)  
- **PoC (Point of Contact)** – Designated communication person  

### R  
- **RAT** – Remote Access Trojan  
- **RTO** – Recovery Time Objective  
- **RPO** – Recovery Point Objective  
- **RDP** – Remote Desktop Protocol  

### S  
- **Sandbox** – Isolated environment for safe malware analysis  
- **SIEM** – Security Information and Event Management  
- **SOC** – Security Operations Center  
- **SOAR** – Security Orchestration, Automation, and Response  
- **SQLi** – SQL Injection  
- **Supply Chain Attack** – Compromise via a trusted vendor or supplier  

### T  
- **TTP** – Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures  
- **2FA** – Two-Factor Authentication  

### U  
- **UI** – User Interaction (in CVSS)  

### V  
- **Vulnerability** – Weakness that can be exploited  
- **WAF** – Web Application Firewall  
- **WASM** – WebAssembly  

### Y  
- **YARA** – Rule-based malware identification and classification  

### X
- **XSS** - Cross-Site Scripting
